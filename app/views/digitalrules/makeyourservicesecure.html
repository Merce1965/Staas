Rule 4 - Make your service secure

Support the implementation of a defence in depth approach to Security.

This rule forms part of the Defence Digital Rules, as embodied in the Defence Service Manual.

Our architectural standards and policies are designed to make your service secure throughout its lifecycle.

Secure by Design principles

To help ensure your service is secure, you should read and understand our Secure by Design principles.

Principle 1: Context

Understand the programme’s cyber-security context and how it will use and manage data.

Principle 2: Planning

Understand the programme’s security workstream and perform initial planning. This includes assessing the cyber threat and defining and validating any security requirements.

Principle 3: Risk Assessment and Oversight

Include cyber security risk management into existing programme governance as a continual process.

Principle 4: Security Control Definition

Define and apply security controls to prevent any risks identified. Reuse existing services and patterns where they exist.

Principle 5: Supply Chain

Understand the role and risks of the supply chain and how to ensure they meet their security responsibilities.

Principle 6: Security Testing and Assurance

Work with cyber security experts throughout to gain security assurance and test and validate throughout the programme lifecycle.

Principle 7: Through Life Management

Monitor and improve security continually. Enable and meet assurance and make a plan for disposal.

What you must do

To design a secure service for now and in the future, you must:

  • follow our Secure by Design Principles
  • follow our Defence Manual of Security and Resilience (part of JSP 440), to ensure your service is accredited
  • appoint a Security Assurance Coordinator (SAC) with a level of expertise in line with your project
  • appoint a Project Security Officer (PSyO) if your project is classified SECRET or above
  • follow JSP 604 rules relating to Security:
    • Rule 9, ICT shall be developed using the cross domain solutions approach defined by UKStratCom CyDR Security Architecture Team, you must engage with CyDR for any solution that has a cross domain information exchange requirement
    • Rule 10, ICT shall be accredited in accordance with current policy, your solution must undergo the accreditation process
    • Rule 11, ICT shall comply with Op AUGITE requirements, your solution must implement measures to comply with Op AUGITE
    • Rule 22, ICT shall be compliant with UK government information assurance policy for the employment of cryptographic products, the solution must engage with CyDR Crypt-Key and complete a Crypt-Key Management Plan (CMP)
  • follow the approaches in the Product Lifecycle Management principles and technical standards (part of the Foundry Way)
  • agree any proposal to use a new network gateway with the CyDR team before you start your project
  • protect all communications using high grade crypto for any system classified as SECRET or TOP SECRET. This must be agreed by the Crypt Key Enterprise Authority and documented in a crypto management plan as described in JSP 490 and JSP 491
  • use network bearers that are fit for purpose and offer good value for the type of service you are designing and its security requirements
  • work with commercial teams to ensure that services delivered by a 3rd party include evergreen commitments. This is to reduce cyber risks from the use of any outdated technology
