Digital Rules

The Digital Rules are a set of criteria to help us design, build and buy technology in the Ministry of Defence (MOD).

The Digital Rules is an agreed standard used for the Ministry of Defence spend control process (coming soon).

We have adapted the rules based on the Government Technology Code of Practice and they are designed specifically for MOD.

You should use these rules for all of your technology projects or programmes. Consider each point, align your project or programme with the mandatory points, and follow as many of the remaining points as is practical.

Where there is no guidance for a specific area of your project or programme, you should follow the Government Technology Code of Practice.

We're testing this latest prototype to understand whether it meets the needs of Defence, and it will iterate based on what we discover.

1 Make use of common standards and patterns

Before you can get approval to proceed with your project, you must submit the idea for your project to the Architecture Triage Board.

Read more about Rule 1 - Make use of common standards and patterns

2 Realise interoperability

When designing a new service, you must plan for current and future integrations with other Defence data and systems.

Read more about Rule 2 - Realise interoperability

3 Recognise data as a strategic asset

Our data should be available to us when it is needed, regardless of our physical location or the device we use to access it.

Read more about Rule 3 - Recognise data as a strategic asset

4 Make your service secure

Our data should be available to us when it is needed, regardless of our physical location or the device we use to access it.

Read more about Rule 4 - Make your service secure

5 Build in futureproofing from the start

Futureproofing describes a range of measures that help ensure the technology we use remains current, supported, secure and reliable.

Read more about Rule 5 - Build in futureproofing from the start

6 Host your service in the cloud

Use this guidance to ensure you meet the required standards for using cloud technology in your service.

Read more about Rule 6 - Host your service in the cloud

7 Accelerate and innovate

We encourage the use of modern technologies, such as Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Robotic Process Automation (RPA), to help us improve our decision-making in Defence.

Read more about Rule 7 - Accelerate and innovate

8 Reuse existing products

You should aim to reuse and scale existing products, to help save time and money when delivering services.

Read more about Rule 8 - Reuse existing products

9 Apply continuous improvement

The best way to build good services is to start small and iterate often.

Read more about Rule 9 - Apply continuous improvement
