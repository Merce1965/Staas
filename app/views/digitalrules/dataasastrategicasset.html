Rule 3 - Recognise data as a strategic asset

Enable sustainable exploitation of data for modern warfare, both from the centre and at the edge.

This rule forms part of the Defence Digital Rules, as embodied in the Defence Service Manual.

Our data should be available to us when it is needed, regardless of our physical location or the device we use to access it.

To help achieve this we must ensure our data is:

  • secure
  • standardised
  • owned and accounted for by Defence
  • discoverable and interoperable
  • reusable

What you must do

To help ensure your data is a strategic asset for Defence, you must:

  • follow the Defence Data Rules
  • follow JSP 441 when storing, processing or managing any Defence information, knowledge or data
  • follow JSP 604 rules relating to Data, specifically:
    • Rule 0, ICT shall be developed against an understood business need, you must clearly articulate the scope of the solution and the business function(s) the solution will support
    • Rule 2, Projects shall maximise their contribution towards the Single Information Environment (SIE), you must confirm whether the solution supports the Single Information Environment (SIE)
    • Rule 13, ICT shall be compliant with information management policy, you must demonstrate how Information Management is being applied within the solution, including how data is being classified and secured from unauthorised access
  • follow Open standards for government data and technology if your service exchanges information. This includes sharing Cyber Threat intelligence, information between emergency services with MAIT and collaborating on government documents
  • follow JSP 465 Defence Geospatial Intelligence Policy and DEFSTAN 00-102 if your service uses Geospatial and Meteorological data
  • follow JSP 939 Defence policy for modelling and simulation if your service uses modelling and simulation data

A set of foundational policies are currently being developed by the Chief Data Office (CDO) to establish a firm foundation for governing our data throughout its life. These Data Management policies are based on Data Management Association (DAMA) best practice and guided by our Data Management Principles. Once approved they will be accessible on the Policies (Data Management) page of the Data Wiki, part of JSP441.
