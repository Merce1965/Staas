Rule 8 - Reuse existing products

Focus on reusing and building from our existing technology components.

This rule forms part of the Defence Digital Rules, as embodied in the Defence Service Manual.

You should aim to reuse and scale existing products to help save time and money when delivering services, and to move us away from our traditional legacy and obsolescence challenges.

If a suitable existing product is not available, and you need to buy or build one instead, you should ensure it can be used by others in Defence by designing common, reusable services and exposing the best of those ideas, standards, patterns and experiences.

What you must do

To help ensure we deliver value and quality in our services, you must:

  • follow JSP 604 rules relating to Reuse:
    • Rule 1, ICT shall be reused where available, you must reuse Defence and Cross Government ICT where available
  • use our existing digital platforms, such as DevSecOps, MODCloud, API Management
  • select products from our approved product catalogue, if a common digital platform is not available
  • services not currently in use in the MOD may still be available under existing contracts, these must be leveraged before seeking to procure or build new
  • follow GovWifi guidance if you are setting up a new Wi-Fi network
  • re-use existing architecture building blocks for your service, for example the Defence Digital Reference Architecture
  • aim to reduce our carbon footprint by choosing more sustainable products where they meet your requirements, favour suppliers committed to achieving Net Zero by 2050

A Core technology reference list (coming soon) is being curated and will soon be made available showcasing recommended products and solutions used across the Defence estate.
