Rule 1 - Make use of common standards and patterns

To guide, support, and cohere technology solutions in the best interests of Defence as a whole.

This rule forms part of the Defence Digital Rules, as embodied in the Defence Service Manual.

You must use approved common standards, technical reference architectures and patterns for your project, whenever possible.

This helps us achieve a standard approach to building services so we can work efficiently now and in the future.

Before you can get approval to proceed with your project, you must submit the idea for your project to the Architecture Triage Board.

We will review your idea and explain what you need to do in order to proceed. The review can take up to 4 weeks, so we recommend you submit your idea as early as possible.

What you must do

When developing a new, or changing an existing digital service, you must:

  • submit your project idea to the Architecture Triage Board
  • follow the guidance in our Common Technology Architecture
  • follow the guidance in The Foundry Way (coming soon)
  • follow the Technology Code of Practice
  • follow the GDS Service Standard where MOD specific guidance does not exist
  • follow the Defence Digital Service Management Framework (SMF) when designing services with Managed Service Providers (MSPs)
  • follow JSP 604 for assurance requirements, technical and product standards, specifically:
    • Rule 3, ICT shall be developed with respect to the relevant domain architecture, solutions must obtain compliance approval from each of the relevant Domain Authorities
    • Rule 4, ICT shall be compliant with quality of service (QOS) policy, your solution must comply with the IP Quality of Service (QoS) directive and implement appropriate QoS measures
    • Rule 5, ICT shall be compliant with domain name system (DNS) policy, your solution must comply with MOD DNS policy
    • Rule 6, ICT shall be compliant with internet protocol (IP) policy, your solution must be IPv4 based and IPv6 compatible in line with MOD policy
    • Rule 7, ICT shall be compliant with messaging policy, your solution must comply with MOD messaging policy
    • Rule 8, ICT shall be compliant with electronic directory services policy, your solution must comply with MOD electronic directory services policy
    • Rule 19, ICT shall comply with spectrum management requirements, your solution must have Joint Spectrum Authority (JSA) approval for any system that utilises electromagnetic transmissions
    • Rule 20, ICT shall comply with electromagnetic integration requirements, your solution must take necessary measures to protect any Electromagnetic Integration aspects
  • follow the approaches in the Network Reference Architecture
  • follow JSP 935 for any equipment acquisition project that has a software element (including FMS, OTS, bespoke and open source)
